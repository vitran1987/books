## Framework 1: AI Funding Readiness Assessment

### 1.1 Company Maturity and Traction Evaluation

#### Technical Readiness Assessment
**AI Technology Maturity Scorecard:**
```
Core AI Capabilities (25 points):
□ Proprietary AI models or algorithms (5 points)
□ Unique training data or data advantages (5 points)
□ Technical performance benchmarks vs. competitors (5 points)
□ Scalable AI infrastructure and architecture (5 points)
□ IP protection and defensible technology moats (5 points)

Product-Market Fit Validation (25 points):
□ Customer discovery and validation completed (5 points)
□ Minimum viable product with user feedback (5 points)
□ Clear value proposition and customer pain point solution (5 points)
□ Early customer traction and usage metrics (5 points)
□ Product roadmap aligned with market needs (5 points)

Business Model Validation (25 points):
□ Revenue model defined and tested (5 points)
□ Unit economics and pricing strategy validated (5 points)
□ Customer acquisition cost and lifetime value metrics (5 points)
□ Scalable go-to-market strategy developed (5 points)
□ Competitive differentiation and positioning clarity (5 points)

Market Opportunity Assessment (25 points):
□ Total addressable market (TAM) sizing and validation (5 points)
□ Serviceable addressable market (SAM) analysis (5 points)
□ Market timing and adoption curve positioning (5 points)
□ Competitive landscape analysis and positioning (5 points)
□ Regulatory environment and compliance readiness (5 points)
```

**Readiness Scoring:**
- **90-100 points**: Investment ready - proceed with fundraising
- **75-89 points**: Near ready - address gaps before fundraising
- **60-74 points**: Development needed - 3-6 months preparation required
- **Below 60 points**: Early stage - focus on product development

### 1.2 Market Opportunity and Timing Considerations

#### Market Sizing and Validation Framework
**TAM-SAM-SOM Analysis for AI Startups:**
```
Total Addressable Market (TAM):
1. Define the broadest market category (e.g., "Enterprise AI Software")
2. Research market size from authoritative sources (IDC, Gartner, McKinsey)
3. Validate market growth projections and trends
4. Consider global vs. regional market opportunities

Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM):
1. Narrow to specific use cases and customer segments
2. Apply geographic and regulatory constraints
3. Consider competitive landscape and market share potential
4. Validate with customer interviews and market research

Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM):
1. Realistic market share achievable in 3-5 years
2. Consider go-to-market strategy and execution capability
3. Factor in competitive advantages and differentiation
4. Validate with bottom-up customer acquisition projections
```

**Market Timing Assessment:**
```
Technology Adoption Lifecycle Position:
□ Innovators (2.5%): Bleeding edge, high risk/reward
□ Early Adopters (13.5%): Visionary customers, proof of concept
□ Early Majority (34%): Pragmatic customers, proven solutions
□ Late Majority (34%): Conservative customers, established solutions
□ Laggards (16%): Traditional customers, reluctant adopters

Market Readiness Indicators:
□ Customer pain point urgency and budget allocation
□ Competitive solution maturity and market education
□ Regulatory environment and compliance requirements
□ Technology infrastructure and integration readiness
□ Economic conditions and investment climate
```

### 3.2 Negotiation Strategy and Term Optimization

#### Term Sheet Negotiation Framework
**Key Terms Prioritization:**
```
Tier 1: Critical Terms (Non-negotiable)
1. Valuation and ownership percentage
2. Liquidation preferences and participation rights
3. Board composition and control provisions
4. Anti-dilution protection and down-round protection

Tier 2: Important Terms (Negotiable)
5. Option pool size and employee equity allocation
6. Drag-along and tag-along rights
7. Information rights and reporting requirements
8. Founder vesting and acceleration provisions

Tier 3: Standard Terms (Market standard)
9. Dividend rights and conversion features
10. Registration rights and piggyback provisions
11. Right of first refusal and co-sale agreements
12. No-shop and exclusivity periods
```

**Negotiation Strategy by Funding Stage:**
```
Seed Stage ($500K - $3M):
- Focus on founder-friendly terms and minimal control
- Standard liquidation preferences (1x non-participating)
- Smaller option pools (10-15%) and longer vesting
- Limited board seats and information rights

Series A ($3M - $15M):
- Professional terms with investor protection
- Liquidation preferences with participation caps
- Standard option pools (15-20%) and acceleration
- Board control or significant representation

Series B+ ($15M+):
- Sophisticated terms with growth provisions
- Complex liquidation and participation structures
- Performance-based vesting and retention packages
- Professional board composition and governance
```
