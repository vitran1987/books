## Framework 1: Regulatory Assessment Matrix

### Presentation Context
**Story Introduction**: Sarah's overwhelming discovery of EU AI Act requirements and multi-jurisdictional complexity
**Expert Guide**: Dr. Michael Chen introduces systematic regulatory landscape evaluation
**Implementation Setting**: Sarah's team conducting comprehensive regulatory assessment

### Framework Components Presented

#### Multi-Dimensional Assessment Approach
**Narrative Integration**: Sarah's realization that compliance requires systematic evaluation across multiple dimensions
**Practical Tools**:
- Geographic scope mapping templates
- AI system risk classification guides
- Industry sector requirement checklists
- Data processing compliance matrices

**Assessment Dimensions**:
1. **Geographic Scope**: Markets and jurisdictions of operation
   - EU AI Act for European markets
   - NIST AI RMF for US federal customers
   - UK AI White Paper for British expansion
   - Emerging national frameworks for global markets

2. **AI System Classification**: Risk level and regulatory category
   - Prohibited AI systems (immediate ban)
   - High-risk AI systems (strict compliance)
   - Limited risk AI systems (transparency requirements)
   - Minimal risk AI systems (self-regulation)

3. **Industry Sector**: Sector-specific regulatory requirements
   - Healthcare: Medical device regulations, clinical validation
   - Financial Services: Algorithmic trading oversight, model risk management
   - Education: Student privacy protection, FERPA compliance
   - Employment: Equal opportunity compliance, bias detection

4. **Data Processing**: Personal data and sensitive information handling
   - GDPR compliance for European data
   - HIPAA requirements for US healthcare information
   - Data localization requirements by jurisdiction
   - Cross-border data transfer regulations

5. **Deployment Context**: B2B, B2C, or government applications
   - Enterprise customer requirements and procurement standards
   - Consumer protection and transparency obligations
   - Government sector security and compliance standards
   - Public sector accountability and audit requirements

#### Implementation Process
**Narrative Integration**: Sarah's systematic approach to regulatory landscape mapping
**Step-by-Step Guidance**:
1. **Jurisdiction Mapping**: Identify all applicable regulatory jurisdictions
2. **Risk Classification**: Determine AI system risk level per jurisdiction
3. **Requirement Analysis**: Map specific compliance obligations
4. **Gap Assessment**: Identify current compliance gaps
5. **Priority Ranking**: Prioritize compliance activities by risk and timeline

## Framework Customization Guidelines

### Company Size Adaptations
**Startup (1-50 employees)**:
- Focus on core jurisdiction compliance
- Leverage external expertise and consultants
- Prioritize high-impact, low-cost implementations
- Build compliance into product development

**Growth Stage (51-200 employees)**:
- Develop internal compliance capabilities
- Implement systematic compliance frameworks
- Expand to multiple jurisdictions strategically
- Build compliance as competitive advantage

**Enterprise (200+ employees)**:
- Establish dedicated compliance organizations
- Implement comprehensive global compliance programs
- Lead industry standards and best practice development
- Influence regulatory development through engagement

### Industry Adaptations
**High-Risk Industries (Healthcare, Finance, Transportation)**:
- Prioritize safety and risk management
- Implement comprehensive quality management systems
- Conduct extensive validation and testing
- Maintain detailed documentation and audit trails

**Medium-Risk Industries (Education, HR, Marketing)**:
- Focus on privacy and bias protection
- Implement transparency and explainability measures
- Establish user consent and control mechanisms
- Monitor for discriminatory impacts and outcomes

**Lower-Risk Industries (Entertainment, Gaming, Productivity)**:
- Implement basic transparency and disclosure
- Establish user privacy and data protection
- Monitor for unintended consequences
- Maintain flexibility for emerging requirements

This framework presentation guide ensures that all regulatory compliance tools are presented within engaging narrative context while providing immediate practical value for entrepreneur implementation across diverse industries and company stages.
