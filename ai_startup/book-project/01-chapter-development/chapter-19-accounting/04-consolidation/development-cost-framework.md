## AI Development Cost Framework Architecture

### Framework Component 1: ASC 350-40 Capitalization Decision System

#### Software Development Cost Capitalization for AI Projects

**Phase 1: Development Stage Classification and Assessment (Week 1)**

**Step 1: AI Project Stage Identification Framework**
1. **Preliminary Project Stage (Expense Immediately)**:
   - **Feasibility Studies**: Technical and commercial feasibility analysis
   - **Research Activities**: Basic AI research and algorithm exploration
   - **Conceptual Design**: High-level system architecture and approach
   - **Alternative Evaluation**: Comparison of different AI methodologies
   - **Vendor Selection**: Evaluation of AI development tools and platforms
   - **Project Planning**: Initial resource planning and timeline development

   **Cost Examples**:
   - Research engineer salaries: $150K-$350K annually
   - Feasibility study consulting: $50K-$200K per study
   - Proof-of-concept development: $25K-$100K per concept
   - Market research and analysis: $10K-$50K per study

2. **Application Development Stage (Capitalize Appropriate Costs)**:
   - **Detailed Design**: Specific AI algorithm and system architecture
   - **Coding and Programming**: AI model development and implementation
   - **Testing and Validation**: Comprehensive AI system testing
   - **Data Integration**: Training data preprocessing and integration
   - **System Integration**: Integration with existing systems and workflows
   - **Documentation**: Technical documentation and user guides

   **Capitalizable Cost Examples**:
   - AI engineer salaries during development: $120K-$300K annually
   - Data scientist compensation: $150K-$350K annually
   - Cloud computing for training: $50K-$1M+ monthly
   - Licensed training data: $100K-$10M+ per dataset
   - Specialized AI hardware: $100K-$10M+ for infrastructure

3. **Post-Implementation Stage (Expense Immediately)**:
   - **Maintenance and Updates**: Ongoing system maintenance
   - **Bug Fixes**: Correction of defects and performance issues
   - **User Training**: Training users on AI system functionality
   - **Minor Enhancements**: Small feature additions and improvements
   - **Performance Monitoring**: System monitoring and optimization

**Step 2: Capitalization Criteria Assessment Matrix**
1. **Technical Feasibility Evaluation**:
   - **Functionality Specifications**: Clear definition of AI system capabilities
   - **Technical Architecture**: Detailed technical design and implementation plan
   - **Resource Assessment**: Availability of necessary technical resources
   - **Risk Analysis**: Technical risk assessment and mitigation strategies
   - **Performance Criteria**: Measurable performance and success metrics

2. **Management Intent and Ability Assessment**:
   - **Formal Approval**: Board or management approval for project completion
   - **Resource Commitment**: Adequate financial and human resources allocated
   - **Timeline Definition**: Clear project timeline and milestone schedule
   - **Success Criteria**: Defined success metrics and completion criteria
   - **Commercial Viability**: Business case and revenue generation potential

**Phase 2: Cost Identification and Allocation (Week 2-3)**

**Step 3: Capitalizable Cost Categories**
1. **Direct Personnel Costs**:
   - **AI Engineers**: Software engineers working on AI system development
   - **Data Scientists**: Data scientists developing AI models and algorithms
   - **Project Managers**: Project management for AI development initiatives
   - **Quality Assurance**: Testing and validation personnel for AI systems
   - **Technical Writers**: Documentation and technical writing for AI systems

   **Cost Allocation Methodology**:
   - Time tracking systems for direct project allocation
   - Burden rate application for benefits and overhead
   - Project-specific cost center establishment
   - Regular cost allocation review and adjustment

2. **Direct Technology and Infrastructure Costs**:
   - **Cloud Computing**: GPU/TPU instances for AI model training and development
   - **Data Storage**: Storage costs for training data and model artifacts
   - **Third-Party Services**: AI development platforms and tools
   - **Licensed Software**: Development tools and AI frameworks
   - **Hardware**: Specialized AI development hardware and equipment

   **Cost Tracking Framework**:
   - Project-specific cloud resource tagging
   - Automated cost allocation and reporting
   - Regular cost review and optimization
   - Vendor cost management and negotiation

**Step 4: Amortization and Useful Life Determination**
1. **Useful Life Assessment Framework**:
   - **Technology Evolution Rate**: Rate of AI technology advancement
   - **Competitive Landscape**: Competitive pressure and innovation cycles
   - **Business Model**: Revenue generation model and customer adoption
   - **Regulatory Environment**: Regulatory changes affecting AI systems
   - **Market Demand**: Market demand and customer requirements evolution

   **Typical Useful Life Ranges**:
   - Core AI algorithms: 5-7 years
   - AI applications and interfaces: 3-5 years
   - Data processing systems: 3-5 years
   - Integration and deployment systems: 2-4 years

2. **Amortization Method Selection**:
   - **Straight-Line Method**: Equal amortization over useful life
   - **Usage-Based Method**: Amortization based on system usage or revenue
   - **Accelerated Method**: Front-loaded amortization for rapidly evolving technology
   - **Hybrid Method**: Combination approach based on system components

   - **Synthetic Data**: Artificially generated training data

2. **Licensed Training Data**:

   - **Academic Datasets**: Licensed academic research datasets
   - **Government Data**: Licensed government and public datasets
   - **Partnership Data**: Data obtained through strategic partnerships
   - **Subscription Data**: Ongoing data subscription services

**Step 2: Data Asset Valuation Framework**

   - **Processing Costs**: Costs of data cleaning, labeling, and preparation
   - **Storage Costs**: Ongoing data storage and management costs
   - **Enhancement Costs**: Costs of data enhancement and improvement
   - **Validation Costs**: Quality assurance and validation costs

2. **Value-Based Assessment**:
   - **Revenue Generation**: Data contribution to revenue generation
   - **Competitive Advantage**: Unique value and competitive differentiation
   - **Market Comparables**: Comparable data asset valuations
   - **Replacement Cost**: Cost to replace or recreate data assets
   - **Economic Benefit**: Economic benefit derived from data usage

**Phase 2: Data Asset Accounting Treatment (Week 3-4)**

**Step 3: Accounting Treatment Framework**
1. **Intangible Asset Treatment**:
   - **Capitalization Criteria**: Assessment of capitalization requirements
   - **Useful Life Determination**: Estimation of data asset useful life
   - **Amortization Method**: Selection of appropriate amortization approach
   - **Impairment Testing**: Regular assessment of data asset impairment
   - **Disposal Accounting**: Accounting for data asset disposal or retirement

2. **Expense Treatment Considerations**:
   - **Immediate Expensing**: Circumstances requiring immediate expense recognition
   - **Research and Development**: Treatment as R&D expense under Section 174
   - **Operating Expense**: Treatment as ongoing operational expense
   - **Cost of Goods Sold**: Allocation to cost of goods sold for revenue generation
