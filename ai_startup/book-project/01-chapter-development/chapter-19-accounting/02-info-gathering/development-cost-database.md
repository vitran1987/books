# Development Cost Database

## Executive Summary

This document provides comprehensive analysis of AI development cost accounting and capitalization strategies based on current accounting standards and industry practices as of July 2025. The database covers software development cost capitalization, R&D expense management, Section 174 compliance, and cost allocation methodologies for AI companies.

## Software Development Cost Capitalization Framework

### 1. ASC 350-40 Internal-Use Software Development Cost Accounting

**Capitalization Framework for AI Development:**

**Stage 1: Preliminary Project Stage (Expense Immediately)**
- **Conceptual Formulation**: Initial AI concept development and feasibility studies
- **Research Activities**: Basic research into AI algorithms and methodologies
- **Alternative Evaluation**: Evaluation of different AI approaches and technologies
- **Vendor Selection**: Selection of cloud providers, development tools, and platforms
- **Project Planning**: Initial project planning and resource allocation

**Typical Costs (Expensed):**
- Research scientist salaries during exploration phase: $150K-$400K per scientist
- Cloud computing costs for experimentation: $10K-$100K monthly
- Third-party consulting for feasibility studies: $50K-$500K per project
- Software licenses for development tools: $10K-$100K annually
- Conference attendance and research activities: $25K-$100K annually

**Stage 2: Application Development Stage (Capitalize)**
- **Design and Architecture**: Detailed AI system design and architecture
- **Coding and Development**: Implementation of AI algorithms and systems
- **Testing and Validation**: Testing of AI models and system validation
- **Integration**: Integration with existing systems and platforms
- **Documentation**: Technical documentation and user guides

**Typical Capitalized Costs:**
- Software engineer salaries: $120K-$300K per engineer annually
- Data scientist salaries during development: $150K-$350K per scientist annually
- Cloud computing for training and development: $50K-$1M+ monthly
- Third-party data licensing: $100K-$10M+ per dataset
- Hardware and infrastructure: $100K-$10M+ for specialized AI hardware

**Stage 3: Post-Implementation Stage (Expense Immediately)**
- **Maintenance and Updates**: Ongoing maintenance and minor updates
- **User Training**: Training users on AI system functionality
- **Bug Fixes**: Correction of defects and issues
- **Performance Monitoring**: Monitoring and optimization of system performance
- **Minor Enhancements**: Small feature additions and improvements

## R&D Expense Management and Section 174 Compliance

### 1. Section 174 Research and Experimental Expenditure Requirements

**Scope of Section 174:**

**1. Covered Expenditures:**
- **Software Development**: All software development costs including AI model development
- **Research Activities**: Research and experimental activities for business purposes
- **Pilot Projects**: Pilot projects and proof-of-concept development
- **Process Improvement**: Development of new or improved business processes
- **Product Development**: Development of new products or significant improvements

**2. Amortization Requirements:**
- **Domestic R&D**: 5-year straight-line amortization for domestic expenditures
- **Foreign R&D**: 15-year straight-line amortization for foreign expenditures
- **Start Date**: Amortization begins in the midpoint of the tax year incurred
- **No Election**: No election to expense R&D costs immediately
- **Mandatory Application**: Applies to all taxpayers regardless of size or industry

**3. Implementation Challenges:**
- **Cost Tracking**: Need for detailed tracking of R&D expenditures by location
- **Classification**: Proper classification of domestic vs. foreign expenditures
- **Documentation**: Comprehensive documentation of R&D activities and costs
- **System Changes**: Modifications to accounting systems for compliance
- **Cash Flow Impact**: Significant negative impact on current cash flows

**Section 174 Compliance Framework:**
- **Cost Identification**: Systematic identification of all R&D expenditures
- **Geographic Allocation**: Allocation of costs between domestic and foreign activities
- **Documentation**: Detailed documentation of R&D activities and expenditures
- **System Implementation**: Implementation of tracking and reporting systems
- **Tax Planning**: Strategic tax planning to minimize Section 174 impact

### 2. R&D Tax Credit Optimization Strategies

**Federal R&D Tax Credit (Section 41):**

**1. Qualified Research Activities:**
- **Technological Innovation**: Development of new or improved AI technologies
- **Uncertainty Elimination**: Research to eliminate technical uncertainty
- **Experimentation Process**: Systematic experimentation and testing
- **Business Purpose**: Research conducted for business purposes
- **Qualified Research Expenses**: Wages, supplies, and contract research

**2. Credit Calculation Methods:**
- **Traditional Method**: Credit based on excess over base period R&D spending
- **Alternative Simplified Credit**: 14% of qualified research expenses over 50% of average
- **Payroll Tax Election**: Election to apply credit against payroll taxes for startups
- **State Credits**: Additional state R&D tax credits in many jurisdictions
- **Documentation**: Comprehensive documentation of qualified activities and expenses

**R&D Credit Optimization:**
- **Activity Documentation**: Detailed documentation of qualified research activities
- **Time Tracking**: Systematic tracking of employee time on qualified activities
- **Expense Allocation**: Proper allocation of expenses to qualified research
- **Credit Calculation**: Optimization of credit calculation methods
- **Compliance**: Ongoing compliance with R&D credit requirements

- **Time Tracking**: Detailed time tracking for direct AI development activities
- **Project Assignment**: Clear assignment of personnel to specific AI projects
- **Cost Centers**: Separate cost centers for different AI development projects
- **Burden Rates**: Application of appropriate burden rates for benefits and overhead
- **Documentation**: Comprehensive documentation of personnel cost allocation

**2. Technology and Infrastructure Costs:**
- **Cloud Computing**: Cloud computing costs directly attributable to AI development
- **Software Licenses**: AI development tools and platform licenses
- **Hardware**: Specialized AI hardware and equipment
- **Data Costs**: Training data acquisition and licensing costs
- **Third-Party Services**: External services directly supporting AI development

**Indirect Cost Categories:**

**1. Shared Infrastructure:**
- **General IT**: General IT infrastructure and support services
- **Facilities**: Office space and facilities costs

- **Legal and Compliance**: Legal and compliance costs not directly attributable
