## Source Verification and Quality Assurance

### Verification Methodology

**Authority Verification:**
- Official organization websites and publications
- Professional credentials and background verification
- Cross-reference with multiple authoritative sources
- Peer review and expert validation where applicable

**Currency Verification:**
- Publication date verification and recency assessment
- Update frequency and maintenance verification
- Version control and change tracking
- Regular source monitoring and refresh procedures

**Accuracy Verification:**
- Fact-checking against primary sources
- Cross-reference with multiple independent sources
- Expert review and validation
- Correction and update procedures for identified errors

### Quality Standards

**Source Authority Levels:**
- **Level 1 (Highest)**: Official regulatory bodies, court records, company filings
- **Level 2 (High)**: Academic research, established consulting firms, recognized experts
- **Level 3 (Medium)**: Industry publications, technology vendors, professional organizations
- **Level 4 (Lower)**: News media, blogs, unverified sources (used sparingly)

**Currency Requirements:**
- Primary sources: Must be current within 2 years
- Regulatory information: Must reflect current law and guidance
- Technology information: Must be current within 1 year
- Market data: Must be current within 6 months

**Completeness Standards:**
- All claims supported by verifiable sources
- Multiple source confirmation for key facts
- Comprehensive coverage of research framework topics
- Balanced perspective with diverse source types
