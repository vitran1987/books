## üéØ Core Content Areas and Research Allocation

### Content Area 1: Market Gap Identification (25% Research Focus)

**Scope Definition and Boundaries**
- **Systematic Market Analysis**: Comprehensive methodologies for identifying unmet market needs and underserved customer segments
- **Technology-Market Fit Assessment**: Frameworks for evaluating AI capability alignment with market requirements and customer problems
- **Competitive Landscape Gap Analysis**: Tools for identifying white space opportunities and positioning advantages
- **Market Sizing and Validation**: Approaches for calculating market potential and validating opportunity attractiveness
- **Industry Vertical Analysis**: Sector-specific opportunity identification across healthcare, finance, education, and emerging markets

**Content Inclusion Criteria**:
- Proven market analysis frameworks with documented success examples
- Systematic approaches that can be applied across different AI application areas
- Tools and methodologies that provide quantifiable market insights
- Current market intelligence reflecting July 2025 AI ecosystem dynamics
- Practical guidance that enables immediate entrepreneur application

**Content Exclusion Boundaries**:
- Theoretical market research without practical application guidance
- Outdated methodologies that don't reflect current AI market conditions
- Industry-specific analysis that doesn't provide transferable insights
- Complex statistical methods without clear business application
- Generic business analysis that doesn't address AI-specific considerations

### Content Area 2: Customer Problem Validation (30% Research Focus)

**Scope Definition and Boundaries**
- **Customer Discovery Methodologies**: Structured approaches for identifying and validating customer pain points and solution requirements
- **Problem-Solution Fit Assessment**: Frameworks for evaluating customer problem severity and solution value proposition alignment
- **Use Case Development and Prioritization**: Methods for creating detailed use cases and prioritizing development efforts
- **Customer Segment Analysis**: Techniques for analyzing and targeting specific customer segments with AI solutions
- **Value Proposition Testing**: Approaches for testing and refining value propositions through customer feedback

**Content Inclusion Criteria**:
- Customer research methodologies with proven track records in AI companies
- Validation frameworks that provide clear go/no-go decision criteria
- Interview techniques and survey methods specifically adapted for AI solutions
- Customer segmentation approaches that account for AI adoption readiness
- Value proposition development tools that address AI-specific customer concerns

**Content Exclusion Boundaries**:
- Generic customer development without AI-specific considerations
- Academic research methods without practical business application
- Customer validation approaches that don't account for AI technology complexity
- Theoretical frameworks without real-world implementation examples
- Methods that require extensive resources unavailable to early-stage entrepreneurs

### Content Area 3: Technology Readiness Assessment (25% Research Focus)

**Scope Definition and Boundaries**
- **AI Technology Maturity Evaluation**: Frameworks for assessing current AI technology capabilities and development requirements
- **Technical Feasibility Analysis**: Methods for evaluating implementation complexity, resource needs, and development timelines
- **Performance and Reliability Assessment**: Approaches for analyzing AI model performance requirements and reliability standards
- **Resource Requirement Planning**: Tools for calculating technical resource needs including compute, data, and talent requirements
- **Risk Assessment and Mitigation**: Frameworks for identifying technical risks and developing mitigation strategies

**Content Inclusion Criteria**:
- Technology assessment frameworks that non-technical entrepreneurs can understand and apply
- Feasibility analysis methods with clear decision criteria and resource estimates
- Performance evaluation approaches that connect technical capabilities to business requirements
- Risk assessment tools that identify common technical pitfalls and prevention strategies
- Resource planning methodologies that provide realistic implementation guidance

**Content Exclusion Boundaries**:
- Highly technical analysis requiring deep AI expertise
- Academic research without practical business application
- Technology assessment that doesn't connect to business opportunity evaluation
- Complex technical methodologies without clear business decision criteria
- Theoretical frameworks without real-world validation examples

### Content Area 4: Opportunity Evaluation and Prioritization (20% Research Focus)

**Scope Definition and Boundaries**
- **Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis**: Frameworks for systematically evaluating opportunity attractiveness across multiple dimensions
- **Risk-Reward Assessment**: Methods for analyzing opportunity upside potential and downside risks
- **Strategic Fit Evaluation**: Tools for assessing opportunity alignment with team capabilities and strategic objectives
- **Portfolio Optimization**: Approaches for balancing opportunity portfolios and resource allocation
- **Decision Framework Application**: Systematic processes for opportunity selection and strategic planning

**Content Inclusion Criteria**:
- Decision frameworks with clear evaluation criteria and scoring methodologies
- Risk assessment tools that provide quantitative and qualitative analysis
- Strategic alignment methods that account for team capabilities and resource constraints
- Portfolio optimization approaches that balance risk and return considerations
- Decision processes that enable confident opportunity selection and resource commitment

**Content Exclusion Boundaries**:
- Complex financial modeling without clear business application
- Academic decision theory without practical implementation guidance
- Generic business evaluation that doesn't address AI-specific considerations
- Theoretical frameworks without validated real-world examples
- Methods that require extensive data unavailable to early-stage entrepreneurs
