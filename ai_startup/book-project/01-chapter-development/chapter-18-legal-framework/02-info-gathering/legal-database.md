## AI Legal Precedents and Case Law Database

### 1. Landmark AI Legal Cases and Judicial Decisions

**Copyright and Fair Use Precedents:**

**Authors Guild v. Google (2015) - Application to AI Training:**
- **Background**: Google Books case establishing fair use for large-scale digitization
- **Holding**: Transformative use of copyrighted works for search and discovery constitutes fair use
- **AI Implications**: Precedent supporting fair use arguments for AI training on copyrighted content
- **Key Factors**: Transformative purpose, public benefit, limited market harm
- **Current Relevance**: Frequently cited in AI training fair use arguments

**Perfect 10 v. Amazon (2007) - Search Engine Precedent:**
- **Background**: Image search engine case addressing copyright and fair use
- **Holding**: Thumbnail images and search functionality constitute fair use
- **AI Implications**: Support for fair use in AI systems that transform and index content
- **Key Factors**: Transformative use, different purpose, technological innovation
- **Application**: Relevant to AI image recognition and generation systems

**Recent AI-Specific Cases:**

**Andersen v. Stability AI (2023-ongoing):**
- **Background**: Class action lawsuit against Stability AI, Midjourney, and DeviantArt
- **Claims**: Copyright infringement, DMCA violations, publicity rights violations
- **Status**: Ongoing litigation with significant implications for AI training
- **Key Issues**: Fair use for AI training, substantial similarity, market harm
- **Industry Impact**: Potential precedent for AI training data usage

**Getty Images v. Stability AI (2023-ongoing):**
- **Background**: Getty Images lawsuit against Stability AI for copyright infringement
- **Claims**: Unauthorized use of Getty Images content for AI training
- **Status**: Ongoing litigation in US and UK courts
- **Key Issues**: Commercial use, licensing requirements, trademark violations
- **Potential Outcomes**: Could establish licensing requirements for commercial AI training

### 2. Patent Law Developments and AI Inventorship

**AI Inventorship Cases:**

**Thaler v. Vidal (2022) - DABUS Patent Applications:**
- **Background**: Patent applications listing AI system "DABUS" as inventor
- **USPTO Decision**: Rejected applications, requiring human inventors
- **Federal Circuit**: Affirmed USPTO decision, AI cannot be inventor under current law
- **International Variations**: Different outcomes in UK, Australia, and other jurisdictions
- **Implications**: Current law requires human inventors for patent applications

**AI-Assisted Invention Guidance:**
- **USPTO Guidance (2024)**: Guidance on AI-assisted inventions and human inventorship
- **Key Requirements**: Significant human contribution to conception required
- **Documentation**: Importance of documenting human contributions to AI-assisted inventions
- **Best Practices**: Strategies for establishing human inventorship in AI-assisted innovation
- **Future Developments**: Potential legislative changes to address AI inventorship
